# Sentiment_Analysis_for_ShopEase_Customer_Feedback
The purpose of this project is to develop a comprehensive sentiment analysis system that transforms raw, unstructured customer feedback into meaningful, structured insights that can directly inform business decisions. 

*** Company Overview ***

ShopEase Europe is a London-based e-commerce leader operating across the UK, France, Germany, and Spain. They offer a diverse range of products, from electronics to fashion, while prioritising a seamless digital shopping experience through innovative tech and efficient logistics. With over one million monthly active users, the company leverages massive amounts of transactional and behavioural data to refine its services. To maintain high satisfaction as they scale, they are investing heavily in machine learning and sentiment analysis to better interpret customer feedback. This data-driven approach allows them to identify trends, improve offerings, and secure long-term growth in the competitive European market. 

*** Sentiment Analysis Summary & Business Case Research for ShopEase Europe ***

 1. What is Sentiment Analysis and How It Applies to E commerce Reviews
    
Sentiment analysis is the use of natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning to automatically determine whether a piece of text expresses positive, neutral, or negative emotion.
In e commerce, it helps companies interpret large volumes of customer reviews, ratings, social media comments, and support messages to understand how customers feel about products, services, and the overall shopping experience.
The project aims to automatically interpret customer opinions, detect sentiment polarity, and identify the key factors influencing customer experiences.
For ShopEase Europe, sentiment analysis transforms unstructured multilingual feedback into structured insights that reveal why customers are satisfied or dissatisfied, enabling more precise improvements.

2. Key Business Problems Sentiment Analysis Will Solve
   
The major business challenges include:

a. Volume Overload
ShopEase receives thousands of reviews daily, overwhelming analysts. The high volume of feedback overwhelms the analytics teams, making manual analysis time consuming and impractical.

b. Unstructured & Multilingual Data
Feedback includes slang, emojis, and multiple languages. Feedback is largely unstructured and submitted in multiple languages making it challenging to analyze using traditional methods.

c. Subjective Manual Interpretation
Different analysts interpret feedback inconsistently. Different analysts may interpret the same feedback differently, leading to inconsistent sentiment classification.

d. Limited Actionability
Ratings alone don’t explain why customers feel a certain way. High level metrics do not provide sufficient depth to understand the underlying reasons behind customer satisfaction or dissatisfaction.

e. Slow Response to Customer Issues
Critical issues go unnoticed or are addressed too slowly. Critical issues may go unnoticed or be addressed too slowly.

f. Competitive Risk
Without automation, ShopEase risks falling behind competitors. The company risks slower response times and reduced competitiveness.

4. Project Objectives & Success Criteria
   
Key Objectives 
•	Build a scalable data processing pipeline
•	Implement advanced text preprocessing (tokenization, lemmatization, language detection)
•	Develop high accuracy sentiment classification models (Logistic Regression → BERT)
•	Identify key drivers of sentiment (delivery, product quality, pricing, UX, support)
•	Generate human readable summaries of customer feedback
•	Build interactive dashboards for real time insights
•	Deploy a scalable, real time system (Streamlit/Flask)
